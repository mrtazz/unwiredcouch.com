I stumbled upon this book in the book store and was immediately intrigued by this idea of “winter” as a global but also personal season of struggle, death, and renewal. The author shares a lot of personal stories of struggle and accommodating the inevitable in her life. And the idea that a lot of people try to pretend winter isn’t happening or at least wishing for months that it was summer. Which is something that rings very true for me as someone who routinely falls into a winter depression around January when it’s been cold and grey for more than 2 months here in Berlin.
